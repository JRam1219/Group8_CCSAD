Math for Global Understanding

By Dominic M. Dial, Marites L. Cui, and Debbie Y. Grafil

Synopsis:

Math for Global Understanding is a comprehensive text designed to help readers connect mathematical concepts to global issues. This book emphasizes practical applications of mathematics in understanding global challenges, with an accessible approach for learners at all levels. Through real-world examples and engaging exercises, it helps readers see how mathematical reasoning can be used to address and solve problems facing the world today.

Overview:

  • Mathematics and Global Issues: Demonstrates the role of math in addressing global challenges such as climate change, inequality, and sustainability.
  • Practical Applications: Focuses on how math is used in fields like economics, engineering, and health care to find solutions to global problems.
  • Engaging Exercises: Includes exercises and case studies to encourage practical thinking and problem-solving using math.
  • Global Perspective: Offers a unique approach by showing how math can be used across different cultures and societies to address worldwide issues.

The book aims to provide readers with the tools to use mathematics as a means of making sense of the world and contributing to positive global change.
